Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

The man with a different approach

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was an Indian freedom fighter and one of the greatest heroes in the Indian History. His views as a freedom fighter were very different from those of Mahatma Gandhi. Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose’s Indian National Army was also known as the Azad Hind Fauj. He was given the title of Netaji in Germany by Indian soldiers in early 1942.

  • He was born on 23 January 1897 in Cuttack, Odisha in a well to do family.
  • His father was Janakinath Bose and mother Prabhavati Dutt Bose.
  • He gave the famous quote “Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom”.
  • He was deeply influenced by the teachings of Swami Vivekananda.
  • Netaji was elected as the President of the All India Youth Congress in 1923.
  • Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose died on 18 August 1945 in a plane crash in Taiwan.
